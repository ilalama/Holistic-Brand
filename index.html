My Story

A practice built on listening

I spent a decade in clinical nutrition before I understood something that textbooks never taught me: the body doesn't need to be fixed. It needs to be heard.

That realization didn't come in a lecture hall. It came quietly, during a year I spent on the Greek coast after burning out on a career that measured health in lab values and compliance scores. I watched fishermen eat with their hands, grandmothers nap without guilt, children run until they were tired and then simply stop.

"Health is not an achievement. It is a relationship — an ongoing conversation between you and the life your body is trying to live."

I returned home and rebuilt my practice from the ground up. No rigid protocols. No six-week transformations. Instead, I began asking different questions: What does rest actually look like for you? Where in your day do you feel most like yourself? What did you eat as a child when someone who loved you was cooking?

Today I work with people who are tired of optimizing and ready to start inhabiting — their routines, their kitchens, their own skin. My office smells like lemon verbena, sessions always run long, and I have never once counted a calorie.

What I Offer

Three ways we can work together

Each path begins with listening and unfolds at the pace your body sets.

Nourishment Sessions

One-on-one conversations about food, energy, and the rhythms of your day. We explore what feeding yourself well actually means in your life — not in theory, but in your kitchen, your schedule, your reality.

90 minutes · In person or virtual

Seasonal Reset

A four-week guided journey through one season of your life. Together we look at sleep, movement, nourishment, and stillness — reshaping each to fit the person you are right now, not the person you were last year.

4 weeks · Weekly sessions + daily check-ins

Ongoing Partnership

For those who want a steady presence in their corner. We meet twice monthly and build something over time — a way of living that doesn't require willpower because it was designed around who you actually are.

Ongoing · Twice monthly sessions
My Philosophy
"The body is not a problem to be solved. It is a story being told in real time — and the most radical thing you can do is learn to read it with compassion."
— Elena Maris

I believe that wellness is not a destination you arrive at but a quality of attention you bring to ordinary life. The meal you eat slowly. The walk you take without a podcast. The morning you spend doing nothing at all and refuse to call it wasted. My work lives in that space — the space between striving and settling, where something gentler and more honest becomes possible.
